BMW M1 PROCAR

2024 - Car Culture: Exotic Envy - 3/5

The BMW M1 Procar is a grand touring race car designed and manufactured by BMW Motorsport GmbH as what was essentially was a racing version of the BMW M1 although few components were shared between the road and race cars. Designed for competition in the BMW M1 Procar Championship, the M1 Procar was also designed to meet Group 4 technical regulations. The cars were powered by a 3.5L M88/1 inline-6 and was capable of producing some 470 horsepower. All cars used in the championship were built to identical standards and were built by either BS Fabrications, Project Four Racing or Osella Squadra Corse. BMW ended the Procar Championship after two years to concentrate on their entry into Formula One, with some Procars later competing in Group B at the 24 Hours of Le Mans where they proved competitive, scoring a few class victories. Fifty-four chassis were built.

BMW M1

2025 - BMW SERIES - 1/5

The BMW M1 is a mid-engine sports car manufactured by BMW from 1978 to 1981. Styled by Giorgetto Giugiaro of Italdesign, the M1 was created out of a collaboration BMW had with Lamborghini to produce a race car for homologation purposes, with this being the result. The car is powered by a 3.5L M88 inline-6 mated to a 5-speed manual; it is the first mid-engined BMW automobile to reach mass production. The car was hand-built; its fiberglass body was manufactured by Trattamento Italiano Resina and its chassis by Marchesi in Italy and the body partially completed at Italdesign, whereupon the car would be shipped to Karosserie Baur for final assembly. 453 were produced, of which 399 were road-going models; this makes the M1 one of BMW's most rare and desirable models. This casting has been released with different names including Wind Splitter, Street Dog and Street Beast. This was the first BMW casting made by Hot Wheels.

2001 BMW M5

2025 - BMW SERIES - 5/5

The BMW M5 is the high-performance version of the BMW 5 Series, produced since 1984. The E39 5 Series, designed by Joji Nagashima, served as the platform for the third generation of the M5, powered by a 4.9L S62 V8. The E39 was the first M5 to use a V8 engine and the first to use aluminum front suspension components and multi-link rear suspension. A total of 20,482 cars were produced from 1999 to 2003. The base of the casting reads 2001 BMW M5 E39.

BMW 635 CSI

2025 - Wild Widebody - 185/250

The BMW 635CSi is a high-end version of the BMW E24 6 Series, introduced in 1978. Designed by Paul Bracq, the 635CSi uses a 3.4L M90 inline-6 making 215 horsepower although engines for the vehicle were upgraded over its lifespan. The 635CSi acted as the replacement for the older 633CSi. Production of the 635CSi ended in 1989 along with E24 production. BMW also took the 635CSi racing, homologated for Group A competition and competing in a number of touring car championships around the world from 1981 to 1985; the cars raced in championships such as the DTM, Australian Touring Car Championship and AMSCAR but it was the European Touring Car Championship where they were most well known. A highly successful car, the 635CSi won 16 titles and a number of major race wins. About 50 chassis were produced. This casting depicts a racing 635CSi developed for the Australian Touring Car Championship as campaigned by JPS Team BMW.

BMW 507

2023 - Factory Fresh - 185/250

The BMW 507 is a two-seat convertible produced by BMW from 1956 to 1959. Designed by Count Albrecht von Goertz, the 507 was conceived by American BMW importer Max Hoffman as a roadster version of the 501 and 502 models. Initially intended to be exported to the United States at a rate of thousands per year, the 507 was a massive failure for BMW, with the company losing money with every 507 built; this got so bad that BMW was on the verge of bankruptcy, having lost DM 15 million by the end of production in 1959. 252 were produced.
