‘96 DODGE VIPER GTS

2025 - THEN AND NOW - 176/250

The Dodge Viper is a sports car manufactured by the Dodge division of Stellantis (formerly Fiat Chrysler Automobiles) from 1991 to 2017. The second generation Dodge Viper, styled by Tom Gale and codenamed the SR II, was essentially a facelift of the previous generation. The GTS was the first Viper to feature a coupe layout, with a "double bubble" design allowing for slightly more headroom for helmets during track days; it also featured a revised exhaust layout and a slightly more powerful 8.0L Viper V10, now producing 450 horsepower. Production of the SR II ended in 2002 following a few incremental facelifts. 10,422 SR IIs were produced, of which 1,166 were 1996 GTS models. This casting features hidden engine detail when taken apart.

2013 SRT VIPER

2025 - THEN AND NOW - 214/250

The Dodge Viper is a sports car manufactured by the Dodge division of Stellantis (formerly Fiat Chrysler Automobiles). The fifth generation Dodge Viper, styled by Scott Krugger and codenamed the VX, was the final generation of the Dodge Viper and introduced in 2012 as a 2013 model. The Viper was originally sold under the SRT brand as the SRT Viper as part of Chrysler's plan to turn SRT into a separate vehicle brand from 2013 to 2014 before it was reconsolidated under the Dodge brand for the 2015 model year onwards. The Viper is powered by an 8.4L Viper V10, producing 641 horsepower and mated to a 6-speed manual. Production of the Viper ended in 2017, with no replacement planned. 2,427 were produced.

´69 DODGE CORONET SUPERBEE

2025 - MUSCLE MANIA - 208/250

The Dodge Coronet is an automobile manufactured by the Dodge division of Chrysler Corporation over seven generations; the cars shared the same bodyshell with different levels of equipment installed. The fifth generation, styled by Virgil Exner and Elwood Engel, was introduced after a hiatus in Coronet production since 1959, with the car downsized from full-sized to intermediate-sized and built on the new Chrysler B-Body platform. The Super Bee trim level was introduced in 1968 as a companion model to the Plymouth Road Runner, mainly powered by a 426 ci (7.0L) Hemi V8. The A12 package was introduced in 1968 with a fiberglass hood with large hood scoop and various other decal packages. The Coronet was restyled in 1970 and discontinued the same year. 27,800 were produced for 1969. This casting replicates a 1969 Dodge Coronet Super Bee with the A12 package. The 2009 Muscle Mania version has a wider stripe than the 2008 versions.

´71 DODGE CHALLENGER

2025 - MUSCLE MANIA - 131/250

The Dodge Challenger is a pony car produced by the Dodge division of Chrysler starting fall 1969 for the 1970 model year. Designed by Carl Cameron, the Challenger had a longer wheelbase and more spacious interior to compete with the Mercury Cougar and Pontiac Firebird introduced around the same time. Challenger production declined sharply after 1970 and halted midway through 1974 due to declining sales. 165,437 were produced. This is a depiction of a 1971 Dodge Challenger with an optional shaker hood and Mopar Go-Wing. The tooling was updated in 2013 for cost-cutting, with the plastic wing now cast into the body.
